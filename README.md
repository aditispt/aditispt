# Aditi Singh, PhD  
**Director, Bioinformatics Core (OmicsInsight)**  
Wayne State University  

Computational immunology | Single-cell & multi-omics | Translational systems biology  

I lead multi-omics research programs integrating bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, CITE-seq, spatial transcriptomics, and functional genomics to understand immune regulation in development, pregnancy, and cancer. My work combines computational infrastructure with translational biology to uncover mechanisms driving immune imbalance and disease progression.

---

## Research Programs

### 1. Pan-Stress Response and Developmental Imbalance  
Identified conserved stress-response programs in embryonic stem cells demonstrating that diverse environmental stressors override naïve lineage identity and drive predictable developmental imbalance trajectories.  
**Outputs:** Scientific Reports (2025) | Birth Defects Research (2024) | Patent filed (2025)

---

### 2. Tissue-Resident Dendritic Cells in the Endometrium  
Built a single-cell atlas of uterine dendritic cells across the menstrual cycle. Discovered seven distinct subsets including progenitor populations, mapped developmental trajectories, and defined functional roles in immune regulation and implantation.  
**Published:** iScience (2025)

---

### 3. Sex-Specific Immune Regulation at the Maternal–Fetal Interface  
Integrated bulk and single-cell transcriptomics to demonstrate early sex-biased immune programming in placenta and decidua. Identified inflammatory skewing in male pregnancies, regulatory bias in females, and distinct immune failure pathways in miscarriage.  
**Status:** Manuscript under review (2026)

---

### 4. Tumor Cell Sialylation and Immune Suppression in Ovarian Cancer  
Combined CRISPR perturbation, flow cytometry, bulk RNA-seq, and single-cell RNA-seq to show that tumor hypersialylation reprograms the immune microenvironment. Demonstrated that ST6GAL1-driven sialylation expands immunosuppressive myeloid populations and that targeting sialylation reverses immune suppression.
