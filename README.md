Aditi Singh, PhD

Director, Bioinformatics Core | Translational Immunology & Multi-omics
Wayne State University

I lead multi-omics research programs integrating bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, CITE-seq, and functional genomics to understand immune regulation in development, pregnancy, and cancer. My work combines computational infrastructure with translational biology to uncover mechanisms driving immune imbalance and disease progression.

Research Programs
1. Pan-Stress Response and Developmental Imbalance

Identified conserved stress-response programs in embryonic stem cells showing that diverse environmental stressors override naïve lineage identity and drive predictable developmental imbalance trajectories.
Publications in Scientific Reports and Birth Defects Research; patent filed (2025).

2. Tissue-Resident Dendritic Cells in the Endometrium

Built a single-cell atlas of uterine dendritic cells across the menstrual cycle.
Discovered seven distinct subsets including progenitor populations, mapped their developmental trajectory, and defined functional roles in immune regulation and implantation.
Published in iScience (2025).

3. Sex-Specific Immune Regulation at the Maternal–Fetal Interface

Integrated bulk and single-cell transcriptomics to demonstrate early sex-biased immune programming in placenta and decidua.
Identified distinct inflammatory and regulatory trajectories in male versus female pregnancies and sex-specific immune failure pathways in miscarriage.
Manuscript under review (2026).

4. Tumor Cell Sialylation and Immune Suppression in Ovarian Cancer

Combined CRISPR perturbation, flow cytometry, bulk RNA-seq, and scRNA-seq to show that tumor hypersialylation reprograms the tumor microenvironment.
Demonstrated that ST6GAL1-driven sialylation expands immunosuppressive myeloid populations and that targeting sialylation reverses immune suppression.
